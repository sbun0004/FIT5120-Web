Why do we need sustainable management?

As the urbanization of any place starts, the amount of waste generated in that place also increases. This leads to the contamination of the environment. To prevent this from happening, switching to a sustainable life is very critical. Sustainable living involves switching to sustainable waste practices. Doing this contributes to a clean and healthy environment which in turn contributes to the human life health and the animal health and welfare. This will lead to decreasing pollution and land contamination levels which will help conserving the natural resources. It is important that we start today for a better tomorrow!

5 methods to reduce waste

1. Take a reusable cloth bag wherever you go so that can prevent from buying polythene/plastic bags.
2. Use reusable bottles/travel mugs to prevent from buying water bottles.
3. Try cooking and not buying takeout. It can help you get rid of the unnecessary plastic containers.
4. Compost the waste and make sure you purchase ecofriendly products as much as you can.
5. Try implementing Reduce, Reuse, recycle in your day to day lives.

5 ways to sort your waste

1. Organic waste/ biodegradable waste dry leaves, food scraps, vegetables/fruits skin peel
2. Separate your inorganic waste into papers, plastic waste, glass waste. Paper waste will include papers, cardboard boxes, food cartons, newspapers, etc. Keep your glass waste separately to make sure it is safe for the operators who handle the waste.
3. Make sure you use a separate bag for hazardous waste like batteries, concentrated cleaners, electrical waste, or infectious waste.
4. Use a separate bin for dumping medical wastes masks, used syringes, medical supplies like left over medicines or post treatment equipment to prevent the spreading of any contagious/ non-contagious diseases.
5. Use a separate bin for metal waste/ scrapes like tins/cans/containers.
